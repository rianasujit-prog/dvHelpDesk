Understanding Domestic Abuse

Domestic abuse is a pattern of behaviors used by one person to gain or maintain control over another in a close relationship. It can include physical, emotional, psychological, financial, or verbal abuse.

Leaving & Safety Planning

Safety planning involves preparing steps to protect yourself, such as identifying safe places, packing essential items, and contacting support services.

Important documents, medications, emergency contacts, and financial resources can help make the transition safer.

Shelters, trusted friends or family, and domestic violence organizations can provide temporary safety and guidance.

Many support services help parents plan safe exits while protecting children’s well-being and stability.

Helping Others

Listening without judgment, offering emotional support, and helping them find resources can make a significant difference.

Avoid blaming language. Validate their feelings and let them make their own decisions at their own pace.

Share trusted hotlines, local resources, and offer to help make calls or find information together.
